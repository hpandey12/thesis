Steps in develop a CFD simulation:
\begin{itemize}
    \item select the mathematical model, defining the approximation to reality
    \item discretisation of the space and equations
    \item analysis of numerical scheme and stability and accuracy
    \item solve the resulting equations and optimize the convergence rate
    \item post processing the result

\subsection{General Form of Conservation Laws}
\subsubsection{Scalar Conservation Law}
Considering a scalar quantity per unit volume, $U$, defined as a flow related property. For an arbitrary volume $\Omega$, fixed in space and bounded by a surface $S$,
the general form of the conservation law for $U$ is
\begin{equation}\label{consv_scalar}
    \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \int_\Omega U d\Omega + \int_S \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{dS} + \int_{\Omega} Q_V d\Omega + \int_S\mathbf{Q_{S}}\cdot\mathbf{dS}.
\end{equation}
