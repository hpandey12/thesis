        \item \textbf{simulation details: } the instability is simulated on a 2D rectangular domain, $[0, x_{L}] \cross [0, 4x_{L}]$, where $x_{L}$ is the
        reference length scale. The initial interface, located at $y_{0}$, represents a single-mode perturbation with amplitude, $A = 0.1 x_{L}$, and wavelength, $\lambda = x_{L}$.
        \begin{equation*}
            y_{0} = 2 x_{L} + A \cos(\frac{2\pi}{x_{L}})
        \end{equation*}
        The lower limit of the high Atwood number regime requires the Atwood number to be atleast one half~\cite{HutchinsonDISSERTATIONSUBMITTEDFACULTY}.
        Gravitational acceleration acts in a downward direction with a magnitude of $g = 1.0$. Slip boundary conditions are used for all flow quantities at the top and bottom
        boundaries of the domain. The left and right boundaries are extended using periodic boundary conditions. The physical parameters are selected in order to
        make the simulations dynamically similar to the ones studied in~\cite{hamzehlooDirectNumericalSimulations2021}.
        According to~\cite{MIRJALILI2019221}, a reasonable solution to the RT instability with $At \approx 0.75$ can be obtained with a $128 \cross 512$ mesh.
        With similar mesh resolutions being used in~\cite{hamzehlooDirectNumericalSimulations2021, HuangConsistentEssentiallyConservative2020} to study
        similar RT instabilities, in addition to a mesh convergence study being performed, the domain is discretised using a uniform trimmer mesh
        with size $\delta_{h} = 0.0045$. 
        The timestep for the simulations is selected as $\delta t = 2.5 \cross 10e-4/\sqrt{At}$ similar to~\cite{DingDiffuseInterfaceModel2007,hamzehlooDirectNumericalSimulations2021}.
        The following cases are studied:

            \hline
            1 & $0.5$ & $300$ & $1e-5$ & $0.05$\\
            2 & $0.5$ & $300$ & $1e-5$ $0.007$\\
            3 & $0.5$ & $300$ & $1e-5$ & $0.0045$\\
            4 & $0.5$ & $300$ & $1e-5$ & $0.0025$\\

            6 & $0.5$ & $1000$ & $1e-5$ & $0.0045$\\
